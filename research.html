Work in Progress

  • "The Effect of Immigration Enforcement on Demographic Composition and Labor Market Outcomes"
    2025
    Abstract
    This paper examines the effects of immigration enforcement on county-level demographic composition and labor market outcomes in the United States. The empirical analysis exploits plausibly exogenous variation arising from the activation and expansion of immigration detention facilities. A matching-based staggered difference-in-differences framework is employed using administrative enforcement records linked to county-level demographic and labor market data. The identification strategy accounts for spatial spillovers across neighboring counties and commuting zones, yielding causal estimates of the local impacts of place-based immigration enforcement.
  • "Crime Risk, School Choice, and Enrollment: A Structural Analysis"
    2025
    Abstract
    This paper estimates a structural model of school choice that incorporates crime risk as a key determinant of parental decision-making. Using detailed data on Chicago public schools and geocoded crime incidents, the analysis examines how local crime exposure affects school choice probabilities and enrollment outcomes. The results highlight the role of neighborhood safety in shaping parental preferences and student sorting across schools.
  • "Heterogeneity in Household Energy Income Elasticity Using Quantile Panel-IV"
    Master Thesis, 2022
    Abstract
    This thesis investigates heterogeneity in household energy demand across the income distribution using quantile panel instrumental variables methods.
