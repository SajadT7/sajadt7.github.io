Working Papers

The Effect of Immigration Enforcement on Demographic Composition and Labor Market Outcomes

2025

This paper examines how immigration enforcement policies affect demographic composition and labor market outcomes. Using causal inference methods and administrative data, I analyze the impacts of enforcement actions on local labor markets and community demographics.

Crime Risk, School Choice, and Enrollment: A Structural Analysis

2025

This study develops and estimates a structural model of school choice that incorporates crime risk as a key factor in parental decision-making. The analysis provides insights into how safety concerns influence educational access and enrollment patterns.

Heterogeneity in Household Energy Income Elasticity Using Quantile Panel-IV

Master Thesis, 2022

This thesis investigates heterogeneity in household energy demand across the income distribution using quantile panel instrumental variables methods. The findings reveal important distributional effects that are masked by traditional mean regression approaches, with implications for energy policy design.
