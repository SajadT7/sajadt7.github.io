Welcome to my website!

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My research interests lie in Applied Econometrics, Public Economics, and Energy Economics.

My work leverages causal inference methods, causal machine learning, and structural modeling to explore how policies influence demographic composition, educational choices, and energy consumption patterns. By generating evidence-based insights, I aim to inform the design of more effective and equitable public and economic policies.

Working Papers

The Effect of Immigration Enforcement on Demographic Composition and Labor Market Outcomes

2025

This paper examines how immigration enforcement policies affect demographic composition and labor market outcomes using causal inference methods and administrative data.

Crime Risk, School Choice, and Enrollment: A Structural Analysis

2025

This study develops and estimates a structural model of school choice that incorporates crime risk as a key factor in parental decision-making.

Heterogeneity in Household Energy Income Elasticity Using Quantile Panel-IV

Master Thesis, 2022

This thesis investigates heterogeneity in household energy demand across the income distribution using quantile panel instrumental variables methods.
