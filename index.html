Welcome!

I'm a third-year Ph.D. student in Economics at University of California, Santa Cruz.

My research interests span applied econometrics, public economics, and labor economics, with a central focus on immigration enforcement and its impacts on local labor markets. My work examines the economic and demographic consequences of immigration policy, crime, and school choice, using causal inference methods to evaluate policy effects in immigration, labor, and public economics.

I was also a Research Assistant at Sharif University of Technology. My work involved data analysis and structural modeling in the context of energy subsidy reform.
