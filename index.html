Welcome!

I'm a third-year Ph.D. student in Economics at University of California, Santa Cruz.

My research interests are in applied econometrics, public economics, and labor economics, with a particular focus on immigration enforcement and its effects on local labor markets. I study the economic and demographic impacts of immigration policy, crime, and school choice, using modern causal inference methods to evaluate policy effects.

I was also a Research Assistant at Sharif University of Technology. My work involved data analysis and structural modeling in the context of energy subsidy reform.
